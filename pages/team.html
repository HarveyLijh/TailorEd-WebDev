Julia Voss

Associate Professor of Rhetoric & Composition

Julia Voss is an associate professor of rhetoric and composition and former director of professional writing in the English Department at Santa Clara University, where she researches curriculum/assessment design and equity, learning space design, and writing program administration. Her work has appeared in Computers and Composition, Across the Disciplines, Composition Studies, College Composition and Communication, WPA: Writing Program Administration, and other venues. Navid Shaghaghi

Lecturer and Researcher of Mathematics and Computer Science

Navid Shaghaghi is a lecturer and researcher in the department of Mathematics and Computer Science (MCS) of the College of Arts and Sciences, the departments of Computer Science & Engineering (CSEN) and Bioenginneering (BIOE) of the School of Engineering, and the department of Information Systems and Analytics (ISA) of the School of Business at Santa Clara University. Professor Shaghaghi currently manages 19 research projects and oversees over seventy research students and alumni in his Ethical, Pragmatic, and Intelligent Computing (EPIC) and Creative, Augmented, and Virtual Environments (CAVE) research laboratories as well as under his fellowship roles with with the Frugal Innovation Hub (FIH) and the BioInnovation and Design Lab at the university.

Andres Calle

Neural Networks Team

Andres Calle first entered SCU as a transfer student, switching his major from Biology to Computer Science. After first meeting Professor Shaghaghi in his second quarter at SCU, he went on to work as a research assistant over the summer on various projects including TailorEd. Andres was brought on to TailorEd due to his interest in Machine Learning, and together with a few of his peers, they began to develop CCID, the computer vision program that TailorEd would use to classify the thousands of images that Dr. Voss had gathered by that time. Since that summer, Andres has completed his undergraduate degree in SCU and has moved on to pursuing his masters, working as a Research Assistant to the Bioinnovation Lab and still working very closely with the EPIC Laboratory.
